SYNOPSIS

When a panicked call from their teammate Ryuu leaves behind more questions than answers, a group of student filmmakers stumbles upon a strange camera abandoned in an old classroom.

What begins as a routine project soon takes a disturbing turn when they discover unsettling images and unexplainable occurrences tied to the camera.

As they attempt to carry on, the line between reality and something far darker begins to blur. Shadows move where they shouldn't, whispers echo in empty rooms—and one by one, things start to change.

Some cameras capture memories. This one captures something else entirely.

PRODUCTION NOTES

Director's Vision: Brandon Alvaro Haryanto brings his unique perspective on psychological horror, blending traditional filmmaking techniques with modern digital effects to create an unsettling atmosphere that lingers long after the credits roll.

The Entity: The mysterious figure known only as "The Entity" was brought to life through a combination of practical effects, motion capture, and the haunting performance of Kenneth Andrew Lukita, whose identity remains shrouded in mystery.

Cinematography: Lead cinematographer Ricky Atha Ajie Alvianto utilized vintage camera equipment and experimental techniques to achieve the film's distinctive visual style, creating an authentic found-footage aesthetic that blurs the line between reality and nightmare.

"We wanted to create something that felt real, something that could actually happen. The camera becomes a character itself, and the audience becomes complicit in the horror." - Marcus Thompson, Director
